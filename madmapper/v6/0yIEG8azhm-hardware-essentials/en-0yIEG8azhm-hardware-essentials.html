What You Need to Run MadMapper!

MadMapper is designed to be lightweight, flexible, and compatible with a wide range of hardware configurations.

Whether you’re working on a small projection mapping project or a large-scale installation, here’s what you need to know about supported systems and recommended setups.

Supported Platforms

MadMapper v6 runs seamlessly on both macOS and Windows.

macOS: version 10.9 or higher

Windows: version 10 or higher

 

There is no “better” platform, both systems are fully supported and deliver excellent performance.

The choice usually depends on your personal preference, budget, and the type of projects you plan to create.

macOS Recommendations

MadMapper performs well even on modest Apple hardware, making it an excellent choice for portability and reliability.

  • Entry-level MacBooks (such as MacBook Air or older MacBook Pro models) are perfectly fine for comnon projection mapping tasks.

  • If you plan to work extensively with heavy shaders, multiple video inputs, large 3D content, etc. you’ll benefit from a more powerful GPU (graphics card).

  • Even a second-hand MacBook can be suitable for light to medium use.

MadMapper has been a native M-software for Apple Silicon (M-series) computers since version 5 and above.

Output considerations (macOS)

Modern MacBooks often feature limited video outputs (USB-C or Thunderbolt).

To connect multiple projectors or displays, you might need an adapter or splitter, such as:

  • Matrox TripleHead2Go

  • Datapath FX4 (a more advanced and reliable option)

  • StarTech multi-output adapters (affordable and practical)

Think of these devices as “video multipliers” that expand your available outputs.

MadMapper supports unlimited outputs with any license, the only limitation comes from your hardware setup.

Windows Recommendations

MadMapper runs equally well on Windows systems, as long as they’re properly configured.

  • Processor (CPU): Choose a recent, high-performance model (Intel i5/i7/i9 or AMD Ryzen series). Avoid entry-level CPUs, which can bottleneck performance.
  • Graphics Card (GPU): A dedicated GPU is essential. NVIDIA and AMD cards are both supported.
  • Memory (RAM): At least 8 GB is required; 16 GB or more is recommended for smooth performance.
  • Storage: An SSD drive is strongly recommended for faster project loading and video playback.

Even compact PCs such as Intel NUCs or mini PCs can handle projection mapping surprisingly well, it can be ideal for small or portable setups.
